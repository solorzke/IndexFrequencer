2010 has seen many sequels and prequels in video games, including several new titles such as Ace Attorney, Alien vs. Predator, Army of Two, Assassin's Creed, Battlefield, BioShock, Call of Duty, Castlevania, Civilization, Crackdown, Donkey Kong, Dead Rising, Fable, Fallout, God of War, Gran Turismo, Halo, Just Cause, Kane and Lynch, Lost Planet, Mafia, Mass Effect, Medal of Honor, Mega Man, Metroid, Need for Speed, Pokémon , Prince of Persia, Red Steel, Red Dead, Skate, StarCraft, Star Wars: The Force Unleashed, Super Mario, Tom Clancy's H.A.W.X, Tom Clancy's Splinter Cell and Total War. In addition, it saw the release of many new intellectual properties, such as Alan Wake, Darksiders, Deadly Premonition, Heavy Rain and Metro 2033. Many awards went to games such as Red Dead Redemption, Mass Effect 2, God of War III and Super Mario Galaxy 2. Kinect from Microsoft Game Studios for the Xbox 360 was also released this year.

Events[edit]

Month Day Event
January 7-10 International Consumer Electronics Show 2010 was held in Las Vegas, Nevada, USA[1]
February 11-14 Microsoft X10 held in San Francisco[2]
24-27 Nintendo World Summit held in San Francisco[3]
March 9-13 Game Developers Conference 2010 held in San Francisco, CA[4]
26-28 First PAX East Coast at the John B. Hynes Veterans Memorial Convention Center in Boston, MA
April 15[5] Xbox Live for original Xbox console and games discontinued[6]
20-23 Captivate, Capcom's annual tradeshow was held in Honolulu, Hawaii[7]
May 4-7 The Humble Indie Bundle, an experiment into indie, charitable, cross-platform, DRM-free digital game distribution, is first released to great success.
June 15-17 E3 2010 is held at the Los Angeles Convention Center.[8]
17-19 OnLive released.
August 12-15 QuakeCon was held in the Hotel Hilton Anatole, in Dallas, Texas, USA.
16-18 Game Developers Conference was held in Europe in Cologne.
18-22 Gamescom 2010 was held at Cologne, Germany
September 3-5 Penny Arcade Expo 2010 was held at Washington State Convention Center in Seattle.
15-21 PlayStation Move was launched in North America, Europe and various Asian countries.
16-19 Tokyo Game Show 2010 was held at Makuhari Messe in Japan.
October 22-23 BlizzCon 2010 was held at Anaheim Convention Center in Anaheim, California.
November 4-7 Microsoft's Kinect launched worldwide, starting in North America.
